01 — Research & evidence

Clinical studies &
translational science.

Breathe Belt feasibility

Pilot RCT in post-op & ventilated patients: increased chest expansion, safe usage, improved breathing pattern (p=0.004). First device of its kind for intubated patients.

🔬 2022 · SUTH Innovation Award
Slow loaded breathing in HTN + T2DM

8-week sham-controlled trial (n=17). Resting BP, HRV, exercise pressor response not significantly changed. Negative result adds crucial evidence to non-pharm management.

📄 Master’s thesis (KKU) · TCTR20190723001
6MWT vs 5M gait speed in cardiac rehab

Cross-sectional study NYHA II/III. Strong correlation (Kappa 0.87), suggesting 5M walk test as practical alternative in Thai hospital settings.

📊 Srinagarind Med J · Preliminary report 2022
Exercise pressor response in elderly DM+HT

Augmented BP & HR response during static exercise, but not during PECO — indicating vascular rather than cardiac limitation.

03 — Clinical implementation

Translating evidence into
daily rehabilitation practice.

5-meter walk test

Adopted in local cardiac rehabilitation protocol as a surrogate for 6MWT when corridor space limited. Reduces assessment time by 80%.

Breathe Belt pilot in SUT Hospital

Used in post-surgical and ventilated patients (n=20). Zero adverse events, patient satisfaction 4/5. Currently implementing Phase II clinical protocol.

Slow loaded breathing

Despite neutral BP effect, protocol improved inspiratory muscle endurance & patient self-efficacy. Now integrated as adjunct therapy for breathlessness.

04 — Clinical philosophy

Principles that guide
rehabilitation & innovation.

01
Motor learning cannot be bypassed — only optimized. Technology must enhance neuroplasticity, not substitute it.
02
Adaptive assistance over passive correction. Progressive challenge within the zone of proximal recovery.
03
Movement variability as a therapeutic target. Controlled variability reflects natural motor control dynamics.
04
Technology serves the human, not the protocol. Patient-device interaction requires skilled interpretation.
05
Evidence informs; clinical reasoning decides. Translational rehabilitation balances research, biology, goals, environment.
06
Rehabilitation systems are designed, not discovered. From assessment frameworks to outcome measurement.
“The next frontier of rehabilitation is not a device — it is the science of knowing when, how, and for whom to deploy one.”
