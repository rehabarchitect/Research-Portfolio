About

Clinical Depth
Meets Research Rigor

I am a licensed physiotherapist (License No. ก.9573) at the งานกายภาพบำบัด ศูนย์เวชศาสตร์ฟื้นฟู สภากาชาดไทย — the Thai Red Cross Rehabilitation Center in Bangkok — where I specialize in neurological, cardiopulmonary, and geriatric rehabilitation.

My clinical journey began in Isan, and has taken me through Suranaree University of Technology Hospital, the BDMS Group, and now the Thai Red Cross — each environment sharpening a different dimension of my practice. My M.Sc. in Physiotherapy (Research Program) from Khon Kaen University gave me the methodological foundation to ask better questions and design more rigorous studies.

What drives me is the space between what the textbook says and what the patient in front of you actually needs. I specialize in that gap. Whether it's the first time a ASIA A spinal cord injury patient takes overground steps in a robotic exoskeleton, or a cardiac surgery patient walking the length of the ward for the first time post-operatively — these moments are not accidents. They are the result of evidence, technology, and clinical intuition working in concert.

Beyond the clinic, I develop clinical decision-support tools, supervise physiotherapy interns, and serve as a part-time lecturer at Nakhon Ratchasima College — because the next generation of clinicians deserves better teaching tools than what existed when I trained.

Clinical Milestone · 2024–2025

EksoNR Overground Exoskeleton Program: SCI Reclassification Case

The most clinically significant milestone in the Thai Red Cross RAGT program: a patient initially classified as ASIA A (motor and sensory complete) achieved documented ASIA B reclassification following a systematic robotic gait training program beginning September 2024.

This case involves navigating complex clinical challenges including bow-leg deformity, fibular head bursitis-like swelling after EksoNR trial, and optimizing between HIWIN treadmill training and EksoNR overground ambulation to maximize neuroplasticity while protecting joint integrity.

1

Sept 2024 — RAGT Program Initiated

Patient enrolled with ASIA A classification. HIWIN treadmill-based RAGT commenced as primary platform.

2

Progressive HIWIN Sessions

10+ HIWIN sessions completed. Gradual neuromuscular responses noted. Protocol refined across diagnostic groups (SCI C–T levels, TBI, Stroke).

3

EksoNR Overground Trial

First EksoNR overground ambulation session attempted. Post-session: bow-leg pressure + fibular head swelling identified. Cold compress resolves. Patient returned to HIWIN protocol.

4

ASIA A → ASIA B Reclassification

Documented neurological improvement confirmed. Sensory preservation established below the level of injury. Preparation for academic dissemination (conference → case report).
